## When To Use

Use when the user says:

- "像我写的"
- "按我的风格"
- "保留原文风格"
- "模仿这个作者"
- "不要改成通用 AI 文"

Also use when the user provides prior writing samples.

## Extraction Checklist

Read the sample and extract:

### Sentence Rhythm

- Average sentence length.
- Short/medium/long sentence mix.
- Whether long sentences chain clauses or stack modifiers.
- Whether short sentences are used for emphasis, transition, or conclusion.

### Paragraph Shape

- Thick paragraphs or short paragraphs.
- One idea per paragraph or layered paragraphs.
- Common paragraph openings: fact, scene, claim, concession, question.
- Common endings: cold judgment, open tension, concrete detail, summary.

### Register

- Formal, conversational, academic, technical, essayistic, sharp, restrained.
- Uses first person? Uses second person?
- Uses rhetorical questions?
- Uses humor, irony, or understatement?

### Vocabulary

- Recurring words and phrases.
- Terms the author treats as stable.
- Words the author would not use.
- Level of abstraction.

### Argument Movement

- Fact-first or claim-first.
- Uses examples before concepts or concepts before examples.
- Uses analogy or avoids analogy.
- States uncertainty or writes declaratively.

### Punctuation

- Colon, semicolon, parentheses, quotation marks, dash habits.
- Markdown habits: headings, bullets, bold.

## Application Rules

- Reproduce HOW the author moves, not WHAT the author has said.
- Keep the user's current content and facts.
- Do not overuse a visible tic.
- Prefer structural resemblance over surface imitation.
- Do not add personal anecdotes unless the original task allows it.

## Insufficient Samples

If the sample is too short, use low-confidence calibration:

- Match broad register only.
- Do not infer forbidden words aggressively.
- Do not claim the rewrite is fully in the author's voice.

## Anti-Caricature Checks

Before final output, ask:

1. Did I turn a style marker into a gimmick?
2. Did I add claims or emotions not in the source?
3. Did I preserve the author's normal level of abstraction?
4. Does the text still answer the user's actual task?
